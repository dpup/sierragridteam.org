Two wildfires burned in the Highway 4 / 49 foothill corridor in early July. Both stayed small, both are now fully contained, and CAL FIRE has resolved both. This is a look back at the official figures, drawn from the same feed the [Live Feed](/live) uses; there is nothing active to act on here.

## Priest Fire — Old Priest Grade at Highway 120, Big Oak Flat (Tuolumne)

Reported on July 6, the Priest Fire reached **9.5 acres, 100% contained**, and is resolved, at a final severity of MINOR. It never grew past about ten acres and was fully contained within a couple of days of ignition. The authoritative record is CAL FIRE's [Priest Fire incident page](https://www.fire.ca.gov/incidents/2026/7/6/priest-fire/).

## Landrum Fire — Landrum Street, Plymouth (Amador)

Reported on July 4 just off the corridor's northern edge, the Landrum Fire reached **12.5 acres, 100% contained**, and is resolved, also MINOR. It held near full containment for its last days before closing out. The authoritative record is CAL FIRE's [Landrum Fire incident page](https://www.fire.ca.gov/incidents/2026/7/4/landrum-fire/).

## What it shows

Two ignitions, roughly twenty-two acres between them, both held small and contained without significant spread — an unremarkable early-July stretch, which is the good kind. For radio operators and residents, the takeaway is the ordinary one: the tools that matter are the ones you can reach when cell service can't, and the place to watch conditions in real time is the Live Feed, not a blog post. During either fire, current status lived on CAL FIRE's incident pages and the [Live Feed](/live); for evacuation information the authority is Cal OES / [Genasys](https://protect.genasys.com/).

Figures here are CAL FIRE's final numbers as carried by the S.I.E.R.R.A data feed; where a live incident and a look-back ever disagree, the incident pages above are authoritative.
